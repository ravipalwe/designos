# Direction — Paper Ledger

**Chosen:** Paper Ledger — a well-kept book of accounts: warm paper, ink, ruled lines. Permanence over dashboard flash.

**Signature:** Ledger hairlines — every number sits on ruled paper. Hairlines are the structural system of the whole product: rows, section rules, form fields.

## Why it fits
The brief's dominant axis is calm 5 — money is stressful, the product must not be. Paper Ledger reads as kept, not monitored: warm neutrals lower the temperature, the serif display gives numbers gravity, and the ruled-line motif turns a transaction list (the product's most-seen surface) into the identity itself. It is also the most anti-generic of the three candidates — nobody defaults to warm paper and oxide.

## Design principles (minted at this gate — each one can lose)
1. **The ledger is the interface.** Hairlines and aligned columns carry structure; boxes, fills, and shadows don't. If a container needs a shadow to read, the alignment failed first.
2. **Calm until money moves.** Color marks actions and state changes only — resting data is ink. Negative amounts are not red; red is reserved for destructive actions.
3. **Every number can be trusted.** Tabular figures everywhere, explicit signs, absolute dates after 48h, cents never dropped. No rounding theater.

## Rejected (recorded so future sessions don't re-litigate)
- **Signal Mono** — dark instrument panel, amounts as the only color. Strong, but dark-native narrows the audience for a v1 and reads more "ops tool" than "kept book."
- **Swiss Account** — poster-scale typography, one red. The red-as-accent fought principle 2 before it was even minted; better as a poster than a daily tool.

## Consequences
- Dark mode: **out of scope for v1** (light-first direction); logged in handoff's anti-invention list.
- Positive amounts: marked with a muted `+` prefix, not green — green is reserved for confirmations (principle 2).
